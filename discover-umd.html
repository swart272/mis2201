Why I Chose UMD

UMN Degree

One of the main reasons I chose to go to school at UMD is that you get a University of Minnesota Degree. I liked the campus vibes and brand name and wanted to take it into my own interests.

Location

I chose UMD because of the beautiful surrounding area. While I still get the UMN degree, I get to enjoy the scenery rather than sitting in Minneapolis.

Price

Along with the education factors, I am able to get a UMN degree with a cheaper cost of tuition. Apart from tuition costs, the overall cost of living is way lower in Duluth than Minneapolis!

What I Love About Duluth

I love Duluth because it allows me to earn a University of Minnesota degree while living in a beautiful, scenic city. The natural surroundings, including Lake Superior and nearby hiking trails, make it easy to relax and enjoy the outdoors between classes. With a lower cost of living than Minneapolis, I can focus more on school and student life without financial stress. Duluth’s small-city feel combined with great restaurants, coffee shops, and year-round outdoor recreation makes it an ideal college town.

Campus & City Highlights

Canal Park

Canal Park is largely a conversion of an old warehouse district into restaurants, shops (especially those dealing in antiques and other novelties), cafés, and hotels. This conversion began in the 1980s as an attempt to use Duluth's rich industrial past, the decline of which had left the city in economic turmoil at the time, as an asset in a prospective tourist industry.

Black Sand Beach

Black Beach, located in Silver Bay about 50 miles north of Duluth, is a unique, popular North Shore destination known for its distinctive black sand formed by historic taconite tailings. It features scenic, rugged Lake Superior views, a nearby campground, and is ideal for swimming, rock climbing, and relaxing.

Ianni Hall

My UMD Experience

One of the best parts of attending UMD has been the freedom to explore, whether wandering campus, checking out Canal Park, or enjoying the natural beauty around Lake Superior. Getting involved in the Investment Club, Accounting Club, Sales Club, and University Honors has allowed me to grow in responsibility, communication, and leadership while connecting with students who share my interests. Between classes, extracurriculars, and hands-on opportunities, I’ve gained practical skills that complement my academics and prepared me for future professional challenges. My advice to prospective students is to take advantage of all that Duluth and UMD have to offer, both on campus and beyond, to make the most of your college experience.
